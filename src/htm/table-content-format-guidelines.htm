AEGEE Content Format Guidelines

Which format is the correct fit for every text content
HTML PDF
Criteria
– The content is just going to be read on screen. – The content is going to be printed or presented.
– The content may change over time or require versions. – The content does not change once it is published.
– Parts of the content may be copied or referenced often. – Content will not be copied or referenced often.
– The content has a technical thematic. – The content has a storytelling thematic.
– The relevance of the content will be mostly short or specific. – The content is aimed to be relevant over time.
– The visual design does not have intrinsic value. – The visual design of the content has intrinsic value and conveys a message or vibe itself.
– Style and position of text and images affects little to none the message of the content. – Style and position of text, images, illustrations and tables affect the effectiveness of the content.
Examples of AEGEE data on each format
– CIA.
– Strategic plans.
– Action agendas.
– Activity plans.
– Policy and position papers.
– Statements and manifestos.
– Guidelines and toolkits.
– Reports.
– CD, Network Commission and other bodies’ decisions and announcements.
– Agora and EPM booklets, minutes and results.
– Any other content which PDF is not the best format. 		– Key To Europe.
– X Years Of SU.
– Other project results.
Content published on
– aegee.org – HTML introductory section on aegee.org (like 1). PDF stored in the Materials Database or Issuu (download for consumption outside the browser must be enabled).
Clarifications
– Some HTML content can optionally be also available in PDF (at discretion of the authors). This is the case for PDFs that have a nice design and cover but do not meet the criteria for PDF as the only format.
– The length of the text and the number of illustrations, charts, tables or images is irrelevant to find out whether the best format is HTML or PDF.
